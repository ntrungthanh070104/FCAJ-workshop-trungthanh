# Report: "Exploring Cloud Architecture and Real-World Applications with Amazon Web Services (AWS)"

### Event Objectives

This event was organized to connect Swinburne students with professionals working in the Cloud field. Through the talks, attendees could better understand how enterprises design, deploy, and operate cloud-based solutions on AWS.

Beyond the technical content, the event also focused on career direction, the current hiring market, the challenges that freshers face when entering the Cloud industry, and how to prepare the right mindset, skills, and relationships for long-term growth.

### Speakers

* **Nguyễn Gia Hưng** - Head of Solution Architect
* **Khang Nguyễn** - Solution Architect, Cloud Kinetics
* **Như Trần** - AWS Account Manager
* **Bành Cẩm Vĩnh** - Data Engineer, Renova Cloud

### Highlights

#### Job Market & Career Trends

This part offered a realistic look at the Cloud hiring market. The speaker explained that recruitment expectations are higher now, especially for junior candidates. Many companies use Cloud technologies, and the demand for Cloud talent remains strong, but not every role is publicly posted. A lot of opportunities come from internal referrals and community connections.

The speaker also described the pyramid-shaped hiring model, where companies often prefer hiring more seniors. Cloud certifications can help at the CV screening stage, but real capability, implementation experience, and working attitude are what matter most. Another key point was that the market is less patient with beginners, so preparation needs to be stronger before entering the workforce.

#### School vs Industry: Same Skill, Different Game

This session highlighted the difference between academic learning and real-world work. In school, data and assignments are usually prepared in advance, clearly scoped, and relatively stable. In companies, data comes from many sources, changes constantly, and often brings more complexity.

The speaker also shared a five-year Data Engineering journey across companies such as Sieu Viet Group, Heineken Vietnam, VNG ZaloPay, GreenSM, and Renova Cloud. That made it clear to me that a Data Engineer does more than work with data; they also need to communicate with different departments and understand business needs.

#### How to Build More "Steve Jobs of Vietnam"

This topic focused on personal mindset, fear, and self-reflection. The speaker raised an important point: many young people have fears, but they do not always name or face them directly. Once fear is understood properly, it becomes easier to move forward more confidently in study, work, and career opportunities.

One memorable part was the story about fearing disappointing parents and turning that pressure into motivation. The speaker also shared how networking helped him apply to AWS, which emphasized how important relationships can be for career growth.

#### AI-Ready Freshers

This session explained what freshers should prepare for as AI becomes more present in every tech field. The speaker emphasized that AI should be used more often to support learning and work, but we still need to understand what it is doing instead of depending on it blindly.

Foundational knowledge was also stressed as essential. One mindset that stood out was: "Pay to learn at school so you are allowed to make mistakes; get paid at work to avoid making them." That reminded me to use the learning phase to experiment, fail, and improve.

The talk also covered five evaluation dimensions for candidates: attitude, knowledge, experience, exposure, and personal qualities. The speaker encouraged students to ask questions, build relationships, work with teams, and take action more often because action creates more opportunities.

### What I Learned

* Better understood the Cloud hiring market and the skills companies currently value.
* Saw the difference between academic exercises and real business requirements.
* Learned that networking is important for expanding career opportunities, especially because many roles are never publicly advertised.
* Understood that a Cloud certification can help at the beginning, but real-world ability and work habits determine long-term value.
* Learned to use AI as a support tool for study and work while still verifying and understanding the results.
* Gained more insight into mindset, learning attitude, and personal development from the speakers.

### How I Can Apply It

* Keep learning AWS services through personal projects to build real deployment experience.
* Use AI to help read documentation, analyze errors, and explore new technologies, while always checking the results.
* Build and publish personal projects on GitHub to show practical capability.
* Join more meetups, workshops, and tech events to expand my network in the community.
* Improve communication, questioning, and teamwork skills because they matter a lot in professional environments.

### Event Experience

#### Learning from the speakers

The speakers shared not only technical knowledge but also practical stories about learning, working, and growing in the Cloud industry. Their comments on the hiring market helped me better understand what employers look for and what I still need to prepare.

The part about AI and learning mindset was also memorable because it helped me rethink how to use AI while studying and building projects. The meetup also gave me a chance to meet other students who are pursuing Cloud, DevOps, and related paths.

#### Lessons learned

In addition to technical knowledge, attitude, initiative, communication, and relationship-building are all very important. If a person only studies theory without practice, it will be hard to meet real-world business expectations.

I also realized the importance of staying curious, adapting to technology changes, and continuously improving myself. Participating in tech events is a great way to refresh perspective and stay motivated.
